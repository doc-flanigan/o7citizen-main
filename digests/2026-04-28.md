# This Week in the 'Verse — Monday April 28, 2026

## Game updates

Alpha 4.7.2 was released on Wednesday, April 22, expanding the Nyx star system — a recently opened third star system beyond Stanton and Pyro. The update adds nearly one hundred new contracts spanning thirteen types of work, including transport runs, combat missions, cargo recovery, resource gathering, and missing-persons cases. Among the returning mission types are Courier and Delivery Pilot contracts, which ask players to pick up packages and drop them off at designated locations — exactly the kind of low-stakes work that helps a new player earn in-game money and learn the game's geography. Some contracts in the Nyx system reward successful completion with blueprints — in-game recipes used later to craft equipment — making the new missions valuable for experienced players as well as newcomers. CIG noted that some courier missions are currently unavailable due to a known issue, and the team is working on a fix.

## New ships and vehicles

The Roadmap update published April 22 moved several items into or toward the upcoming Alpha 4.8 build, which is currently being tested by a small NDA-bound group called Evocati testers. Among the items confirmed for 4.8: a gold standard pass for the Aegis Hammerhead — a large, heavily armed gunship with six crewed weapon turrets designed to protect a fleet from smaller attacking ships. A gold standard pass means the ship is receiving a full visual and functional overhaul to bring it in line with the game's current quality bar. Also confirmed for 4.8 are new hairstyles, G-force-resistant flight suits, and a new system that lets players save their ship's weapon and component configuration so it can be restored after the ship is destroyed and reclaimed.

The Vehicle Command Module — a mechanic being tested in the Evocati build — introduces a new way to fly the Drake Caterpillar, a large cargo hauler. Without a small docked command ship attached, the Caterpillar cannot be piloted at all. The smaller ship connects and transfers navigation control to the larger vessel. This is a new ownership mechanic that changes how the Caterpillar works in 4.8 and beyond.

## What's new for new players

The ninety-odd new contracts added to the Nyx system in Alpha 4.7.2 include beginner-friendly transport and delivery work — the same types of jobs that have always been the recommended starting point in Stanton. If you are new to the 'Verse and have recently made your way to Nyx, you can now find entry-level work there without having to travel back to a different star system. Courier missions are a good first loop: accept a job from the Contract Manager on your mobiGlas, fly to a pickup location, collect a package, and deliver it to the marked destination for payment in aUEC, the game's in-alpha currency.

## What's new for veterans

The Evocati builds of Alpha 4.8 introduced a new mission type called Tactical Strike Groups — a co-operative ship combat mission designed for organized groups of seven or more ships. The mission asks players to assault a fortified asteroid base in the Nyx system, working through several phases that each require different ship sizes and combat roles. A smaller escort fighter handles different objectives than a large gunship, so the mission rewards players who bring a balanced mixed fleet. The main objective structure culminates in an attack on an Idris-class frigate — a large multi-crew capital ship — controlled by hostile forces. Completing the mission earns progress toward tiered rewards. This content is currently accessible only to NDA-bound Evocati testers; it has not yet reached the public game.

## Events and community

**Return of Xenothreat (Alpha 4.8 — Evocati testing only, not yet public)**

The Evocati build of Alpha 4.8 also includes the return of Xenothreat — a recurring in-game faction event. Xenothreat is a hostile in-fiction organisation that periodically launches attacks in the Stanton star system. When the event runs publicly, players can take on five concurrent mercenary contracts: four can be completed alone or in a small group, and a fifth — an assault on a Xenothreat-controlled Idris frigate — is designed for a co-ordinated group of at least ten players. Completing any contract earns progress on a shared reward track that gives out blueprints at milestone points. The Idris assault has a three-hour cooldown after each completion; the other four contracts have no cooldown and can be repeated throughout the event. This event is currently running only for NDA-bound Evocati testers, and a public release date has not been announced. When it does reach the live game, all paid backers will be able to participate.

## Developer news

A CIG graphics engineer named BParry responded to community questions on the official Spectrum forum about the game's in-development ray tracing — a visual lighting technique that simulates how light realistically bounces between surfaces. BParry said the current focus is getting ray traced lighting working well for Squadron 42, the game's separate single-player campaign, before bringing it across to the main online world. On the question of reflective surfaces such as water and glass, BParry confirmed that the high-spec version of the ray-traced global illumination system will handle reflections on metallic and shiny opaque surfaces, but transparent surfaces such as water and glass will continue to use an older technique because of technical limitations with how the new system processes fast-moving visual data.

Source: Official Star Citizen forum post — https://robertsspaceindustries.com/spectrum/community/SC/forum/50259/thread/ray-traced-reflections/8798846

A developer named YogiKlatt, who works on the game's flight model, responded to a player question about the upcoming New Flight Model — a long-term redesign of how ships handle. YogiKlatt explained that in the new model, distributing power to engines will affect how a ship performs, but even at minimum power a ship remains flyable, with the performance difference between minimum and maximum power allocation sitting at roughly ten to fifteen percent depending on the ship. The new flight model has not yet been released to the public.

Source: Official Star Citizen forum post — https://robertsspaceindustries.com/spectrum/community/SC/forum/50259/thread/can-we-combine-acceleration-limiter-and-engine-pip/8780197
