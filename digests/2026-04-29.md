# This Week in the 'Verse — Wednesday 29 April 2026

## GAME UPDATES

Star Citizen update 4.7.2 was released on 22 April 2026 and is available to all players now. It adds nearly one hundred new missions to Nyx — a recently opened star system that <Term name="CIG">CIG</Term> has been building out since the previous major update. The new contracts span thirteen types of work: courier runs, delivery jobs, cargo recovery, ship combat waves, enemy commander hunts, salvage operations, and more. Several of these mission types were not previously available in Nyx at all. Some contracts reward players with blueprints — in-game recipes that let players craft items — instead of purely paying in <Term name="aUEC">aUEC</Term>, the alpha currency used during testing. <Term name="CIG">CIG</Term> noted that some courier missions are currently not showing up correctly and that the team is working on a fix.

## NEW SHIPS AND VEHICLES

Nothing new was made flyable this week. However, the April 22 <Term name="Roadmap">roadmap</Term> update confirmed that the <Term name="Caterpillar">Drake Caterpillar</Term> — a large modular cargo hauler — is receiving a major gameplay change in the upcoming 4.8 update. A new feature called the Vehicle Command Module is now in early NDA testing. A smaller command ship docks onto the Caterpillar, which cannot fly on its own without one attached. Once docked, the command ship pilot takes full control of the combined vessel as a single unit, with access to <Term name="Quantum Travel">quantum travel</Term>, landing, and combat. Undocking reverses the process. This is the first implementation of what <Term name="CIG">CIG</Term> is calling "ship pairing."

The roadmap also flagged several items for Alpha 4.8 as tentative — meaning planned but not committed to a release date: the UltiFlex "Novia" Crossbow, a near-silent precision hand weapon; a "Tailwind" flight suit; and a visual refresh of several existing ground weapons including the Deadrig Shotgun and multiple Kastak Arms models.

## WHAT'S NEW FOR NEW PLAYERS

If you are just getting started, update 4.7.2 is the best reason to visit Nyx right now. The Nyx system — a new region of space with its own stations, missions, and resources — now has courier and delivery jobs designed for players who are new to the game. These are simple transport missions: pick up a package, fly it to a destination, collect payment. They are the lowest-stakes way to learn how the game works, earn some <Term name="aUEC">aUEC</Term>, and explore a part of <Term name="the 'Verse">the 'Verse</Term> that was barely populated before this week.

## WHAT'S NEW FOR VETERANS

The most significant development this week is the Item Recovery system for vehicle loadouts, confirmed as tentative for Alpha 4.8. Players will be able to register their ship's configured weapons and components at an <Term name="ASOP">ASOP</Term> terminal. If that ship is then lost, the registered configuration can be reclaimed. The trade-off: items looted from a claimed ship will eventually stop working — a mechanic the developers are calling "bricking." A <Term name="CIG">CIG</Term> community manager confirmed on the official forum that the goal is to end component duplication exploits. Pledged store ships are automatically registered and cannot be permanently lost. <Term name="EPTU">Evocati PTU</Term> NDA testing of Alpha 4.8 is also underway, with Tactical Strike Groups — a new multi-crew co-operative ship combat mission type — and the return of Xenothreat — a recurring large-scale fleet battle event — both in the current test build.

A <Term name="CIG">CIG</Term> graphics developer named BParry posted several replies on the official <Term name="Spectrum">Spectrum</Term> forum this week about ray-traced global illumination — a rendering technique that simulates how light bounces off surfaces to produce more accurate shadows and reflections.
