# This Week in the 'Verse — Monday 27 April 2026

## GAME UPDATES

Star Citizen is a crowdfunded space game in long-term development by Cloud Imperium Games (CIG). Players fly spaceships, trade, fight, and explore a simulated universe. It runs as an ongoing alpha — a publicly available, unfinished version of the game — and is updated regularly.

Alpha 4.7.2, a content update to the game's online world, was released on Wednesday 22 April. It adds close to one hundred new contracts — paid missions players can accept and complete — to the Nyx system, a recently opened region of space inside the game. The new contracts span thirteen different job types, including the return of beginner-friendly "Courier" and "Delivery Pilot" missions, which invite new players to haul packages between locations and start building a career in transport. Several contracts also offer blueprint rewards — in-game recipes used to craft items — which were previously unavailable in Nyx. Other mission types now available in Nyx include wave-based ship combat assignments, cargo recovery work, and missing persons investigations.

## WHAT'S NEW FOR NEW PLAYERS

If you are just getting started in Star Citizen, the arrival of Courier and Delivery Pilot missions in the Nyx system is the most useful change this week. These are entry-level transport jobs — pick up a package at one location, fly it to another, collect your pay in aUEC (alpha UEC, the in-game test currency used in the current alpha version). They require no combat skill and no large ship. They are a reliable way to learn the basics of flying, navigating between locations, and earning money from day one. The Nyx system is a region of space accessible from the game's main star system, Stanton — the established, busy hub where most players start out.

## WHAT'S NEW FOR VETERANS

Alpha 4.8, the next major update to the game, entered Evocati testing on Tuesday 21 April. Evocati is a group of selected testers who work under an NDA — a non-disclosure agreement, a legal obligation to keep what they see private — and are the first outside the development team to test new content. Multiple test builds were circulated across the week. The key features being tested include Tactical Strike Groups, a new co-operative combat mission type designed for organised teams of players, and Item Recovery: Vehicle Loadouts — a new system that lets players save and recover their ship's configured parts and weapons via ASOP Terminals (the in-game kiosks where players summon ships). The Vehicle Loadout system also introduces a change to prevent a known issue where ships and their components could be duplicated: once a claimed vehicle has been sitting unclaimed for a set period, its components will be deactivated and need to be re-registered before use. The return of Xenothreat — a wave-based invasion event where players defend a station against a hostile faction — is also listed in testing but was marked as not yet ready for feedback in the earliest build.

## EVENTS AND COMMUNITY

**LVL UP EXPO — Las Vegas (now concluded)**
CIG (Cloud Imperium Games) attended LVL UP EXPO at the Las Vegas Convention Center from Friday 24 April through Sunday 26 April. The event was open to the public. Attendees could play Vanduul Swarm — a wave-based combat activity where players defend against waves of alien ships — on stations equipped with specialist flight hardware from controller makers Virpil and eye-tracking company Tobii. A CLX Gaming PC giveaway ran alongside the show, with additional entries available to players who visited the booth in cosplay. A Bar Citizen — the community's name for an in-person informal meet-up organised around the game — was held as part of the official show afterparty on Saturday evening.

**Captains of Industry — In-game event (running now, ends 27 April)**
Captains of Industry is a month-long in-game celebration of the game's industrial careers — hauling, mining, and salvage work. The event runs through Monday 27 April 2026 and is open to all paid backers. During the event, a selection of cargo and industrial ships is available for players to try, and themed cosmetic items are available in the in-game store. Note: the Free Fly period — a window during which anyone could play without purchasing — ran from 9 to 20 April and has now closed.

## DEVELOPER NEWS

The Roadmap Roundup published on Wednesday 22 April confirmed several changes to the development plan for Alpha 4.8. Two items were moved to "Tentative" status, meaning their inclusion in the update is no longer confirmed: the FPS Weapons Art Refactor (a visual improvement pass on a set of on-foot weapons, including the Custodian, Ravager, Scalpel, Coda, and Devastator) and the "Tailwind" Flight Suit (a wearable outfit for the in-game world). A new crossbow weapon called the UltiFlex "Novia" Crossbow also received an updated image on the roadmap; it remains scheduled for 4.8, described as a near-silent, high-power weapon with significant projectile drop requiring precise aim. The Item Recovery: Vehicle Loadouts card was also marked Tentative, though it is actively being tested in the Evocati build this week.

Source: Official RSI blog post — https://robertsspaceindustries.com/en/comm-link/transmission/21132-Roadmap-Roundup-April-22-2026

A CIG graphics developer responding to player questions on Spectrum — the official Star Citizen forum — on Monday 27 April confirmed that ray-traced reflections, a visual technology that produces more realistic mirror-like reflections on surfaces, are currently being developed for Squadron 42 (the game's single-player campaign). The developer stated that performance is the primary focus right now and that bringing the feature to the online multiplayer world — known as the Persistent Universe, or PU — will require additional work after the single-player version is stable. Shiny and metallic opaque surfaces will benefit from the reflections; transparent surfaces such as glass will continue to use the existing system for the time being.
